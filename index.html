Before You Fly

Apps to
Download

Download and set these up before you leave — some require setup that's much easier at home. These will make Japan seamless.

📶
eSIM
Get internet for your trip before you fly! Journey Japan or Ubigi are great options. Much easier than hunting for a SIM at the airport — activate the moment you land.
🛂
Visit Japan Web
Japan's official customs & immigration registration site. Fill it out before your flight and get a QR code that makes clearing the border extremely fast.
🚇
Suica
Japan's transit card — add it to your Apple or Google Wallet and tap to ride any train, subway, or bus in Tokyo and Kyoto. Also works at convenience stores and vending machines.
🍜
Tabelog
The best restaurant review and reservation app in Japan — far more accurate than Google for finding great local spots. Essential for booking dinner.
🎟️
Klook
Book activities, Shinkansen tickets, and theme park entry in advance. Use this for DisneySea (book 1 month ahead!), Tokyo Car Meetup, and Universal Studios tickets.
🌐
Google Translate
Download the Japanese language pack for offline use. The camera translate feature is a lifesaver for menus, signs, and packaging everywhere.
🚕
GO Taxi
Japan's version of Uber — often cheaper and more widely available. Great for getting around cities, especially late at night after dinner.
🚻
Flush
Finds the nearest public bathroom. Japan has incredible public toilets — this helps you locate them. More useful than it sounds on a full day of walking!

Getting Around
Shinkansen Tips
Book tickets on Klook or at any JR Ticket Office. Tokyo ↔ Kyoto is ~2h15m. Reserve seats in advance — especially for April 2nd when everyone travels together. IC Card (Suica) works for all local transit.
🌸
April in Kyoto
What to Expect
Average temps: 10–18°C (50–64°F). Evenings are cool — bring a light jacket. Cherry blossom peak is late March–early April. Occasional light rain; a compact umbrella is smart packing.
